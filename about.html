About Kenshi Yonezu

In 2009, "Kenshi Yonezu", or "Yonezu Kenshi" began posting original songs on Japanese video-sharing website "Nico Nico Douga" under the alias "Hachi" and released "Matryoshka," "Panda Hero," "Yunde Kaikaiite Rasetsu to Kareta," etc. In the VOCALOID scene, his addictive rock sound made his presence felt, and he gained attention not only in Japan but also internationally. He is proud of his outstanding achievements, which have attracted attention not only in Japan but also from around the world. He also creates his own illustrations and videos, and released two albums under the name "Hachi" in 2010: "Bouquet and Funeral" and "OFFICIAL ORANGE.

In 2012, he released the album "diorama" under his real name "Yonezu Kenshi" with his own vocals. The strength of the songs with their unique sound make-up and realistic words were overwhelming and became a topic of conversation, vividly imprinting a freshness that was not present in the music scene then. In addition to writing, arranging, programming, singing, performing, and mixing all the songs himself, he also produces videos and artwork on his own, showing his astonishing talent.

Since 2013, he has taken a live recording format and released two singles, "Santa Maria" and "Mad Head Love / Poppin' Apathy". In the same year, under the alias "Hachi", he released a VOCALOID song "Donut Hole", his first contribution to the VOCALOID scene in 2 years and 9 months.

In 2014, released his second album "YANKEE", which reached No. 2 on the Oricon Chart and won the iTunes annual ranking "Best of 2014" Best Album of the Year. It won the "7th CD Shop Grand Prize." The song "Eine Kleine" was used as the commercial song for Tokyo Metro's 2014 ad campaign "Color your days". Along with this, he decided to face his audience properly and announced his first ever live performance. In June 2014, he held his first live performance, a one-man show called "Premium Live: Homecoming Party".

In January 2015, he released his third single, "Flowerwall," which reached No. 3 on the Oricon charts. The title song was used as the commercial song for the Nikon D5500 TV. In conjunction with this release, he went on his first national tour, "Flower Lily Falling," and performed at his first Summer Festival. In September of the same year, his 4th single "Unbelievers" was released, followed by his 3rd album "Bremen" in October. The album was a triple crown winner, reaching No. 1 on the Oricon Chart, No. 1 on the iTunes Weekly Album Ranking, and No. 1 on the Billboard JAPAN HOT Album Chart. It won the "Excellent Album Award" at the Japan Record Awards. The song "Unbelievers" was featured in a commercial for the MIZUNO WAVE ENIGMA5. Starting in January 2016, Yonezu Kenshi's national tour "Yonezu Kenshi 2016 TOUR / Music Corps" will be held.

2016 was a year of great collaborations and great challenges for him. As an illustrator, he participated in the "Yarikkari Collaboration" project for the 15th anniversary of Universal Studios Japan®︎. He took charge of the official image song for the Louvre Museum's special exhibition "Louvre No. 9: Manga, the Ninth Art" and created a special exhibit of newly drawn illustrations. Yonezu collaborated for the first time with Yasutaka Nakata on the theme song "NANIMONO" for the movie "Nobody," for which Yonezu wrote and sang the lyrics. He also released his 5th single "LOSER/ Number Nine" and his first book "Kaiju Zukan". The music video for "LOSER" challenged a new form of expression and it became a big hit.

In February 2017, he released the single "orion", the ED of the TV anime "March Lion", and in June, the single "Peace Sign", the OP of the TV anime "My Hero Academia". As the theme song for "Magical Miku 2017," celebrating the 10th anniversary of Hatsune Miku, he released "Suna no Wakusei feat. Hatsune Miku" under the name Hachi for the first time in four years, setting a record for the fastest million-selling Vocaloid song on Nico Nico Douga. It was also the theme song for the anime film "Fireworks, Should We See It from the Side or the Bottom?" and released the theme song as "Uchiage Hanabi" by DAOKO x Yonezu Kenshi.

In November, his 4th album "BOOTLEG" was released. The album featured a number of active collaborations and challenging sounds, including "Gray and Blue" with Masaki Sugata, "fogbound" with Eliza Ikeda, and "爱丽丝" with King Gnu's Daiki Tsuneda (co-arranger and Gt.), Margaret Hiroi (bassist), and Yaotakuya (drummer). At the SSTV Awards, he won the BEST MALE ARTIST award and "Gray and Blue ( + Masaki Sugata)" won the BEST COLLABORATION award. A solo tour "Yonezu Kenshi 2017 TOUR / Fogbound" was held from the same day of the release of the album.

In January 2018, he wrote a new song "Lemon" as the theme song for TBS Friday drama "Unnatural". Won the "Best Drama Song Award" from the Drama Academy. It was certified as the "fastest ever 1 million downloads" by the Recording Industry Association of Japan. In August,he wrote, composed, and produced "Paprika," sung by Foorin, as the "NHK2020" Support Song. (Choreography by Tomohiko Tsujimoto and Koharu Sugawara). In October, the single "Flamingo / TEENAGE RIOT" was released, reaching No. 1 on the Oricon Weekly Singles Ranking. "Flamingo" was used as the commercial song for "Sony's completely wireless earphones" and "TEENAGE RIOT" was used as the commercial song for "GATSBY". "Lemon" was also used in a commercial for Softbank. In 2018, "Lemon" topped all the major annual charts, and recorded music video views of 400 million and physical sales of more than 3 million copies, which is a record in music history. The group sang on TV for the first time at the 69th NHK Kohaku Uta Gassen.

From January 2019, "Yonezu Kenshi 2019 TOUR / When the Spine Becomes Opal" was held. His first overseas performances in Shanghai and Taipei were also a great success. In April, he wrote, composed, and produced "Machigasagashi" as a new song for Masaki Sugata. In June, he released "Spirits of the Sea" as the theme song for the movie "Children of the Sea" written by Daisuke Igarashi. In August, he released a self-cover version of "Paprika" as the song for NHK's "Minna no Uta August-September". In September, he wrote "Horse and Deer" as the theme song for TBS Sunday Theater's "No Side Game" and released it as a single on September 11, topping the Billboard Japan weekly ranking. The song was also played at the Rugby WC, and has been heard at many sporting events. "Lemon" also won the top 25 ranking in the first half of 2019, and was the "first ever" to win the top spot in the Billboard JAPAN annual ranking for two consecutive years. It recorded the "fastest ever 3 million downloads" by the Recording Industry Association of Japan. In December, he wrote, composed, and produced Arashi's "Kite" as the "NHK 2020 Song," which was performed for the first time at the 70th NHK Kohaku Uta Gassen.

In February 2020, he embarked on a nationwide tour "Yonezu Kenshi 2020 TOUR / HYPE", which had to be postponed due to COVID-19. In March, it was announced that Yonezu had written "Kanden" as the theme song for the TBS Friday drama "MIU404". The music video for "Kanden" received 1 million views in 1 hour and 50 minutes after its release, setting a new record for the first time in his career. On August 5, Yonezu Kenshi's 5th album "STRAY SHEEP" was released. In addition to the theme songs for TV dramas such as "Lemon," "Horse and Deer," and "Electric Shock," the album also featured the self cover of "Paprika" by Foorin and "Machigasasi" by Masaki Sugata, and hit numbers such as "Spirits of the Sea", "Flamingo," and "TEENAGE RIOT," and a new collaboration song with Yojiro Noda, "PLACEBO + Yojiro Noda", "Stray Sheep" used in the "Calorie Mate" commercial, and "Kampanella", whose music video was released on the day of its release, were also included.

An experimental form, the "FORTNITE x Kenshi Yonezu Virtual Event" was held worldwide on the online game FORTNITE, which boasts 350 million users, and recorded an overwhelming number of views. "Kenshi Yonezu Original UT" was also released worldwide in collaboration with UNIQLO, and the new live performance date during COVID-19 and the global expansion of the goods became a major topic of conversation, including overseas. In November, "Canary" music video directed by Hirokazu Kore-eda from the album "STRAY SHEEP" was released. In December, "Kanden" was featured in an Apple Music commercial. The album "STRAY SHEEP" sold 1 million copies within the first week of its release, and later reached 2 million copies. 46 awards for the year 2020, including the AAC Award and Forbes' "100 Digital Stars of Asia".

In 2021, he released "Daydream" as the theme song for NTV's "News Zero" in January, and "Pale Blue" as the theme song for the TBS drama "Rikokatsu" in April. The single "Pale Blue" was released on June 16 2021, ranking No. 1 on the Billboard Overall Ranking and dominating the Top 3 on the Oricon DL Ranking for the first time ever. In August, the music video for "Shinigami," inspired by Rakugo (a type of traditional Japanese comic storytelling), was released. In the same month, they announced the establishment of the interior design brand "REISSUE FURNITURE" and the release of Room Perfume, inspired by the song, as the first item. Also, a limited-time store "Pale Blue Melt" was launched, selling gelato inspired by the song. During the COVID-19 Lockdown, Yonezu wrote, "I hope that a little enrichment in your room will help you to live your days in good health.”

In January 2022, Yonezu wrote "POP SONG" for, and appeared in a PlayStation commercial. In May, he wrote "M87" as the theme song for the movie "Shin Ultraman. The single "M87," with Ultraman on the album jacket, was released on May 18 and reached No. 1 on the Billboard overall ranking. The movie was also a big hit. In October, Yonezu released "KICK BACK", which was used as the opening theme song for the anime series "Chainsaw Man".
